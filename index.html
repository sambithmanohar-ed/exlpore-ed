    Parts of the brain

    • Frontal lobe: planning, decisions, self-control, speech (Broca’s).
    • Parietal lobe: touch, spatial maps, attention to space.
    • Temporal lobe: hearing, language (Wernicke’s), memory.
    • Occipital lobe: vision processing.
    • Hippocampus: forms new episodic memories.
    • Amygdala: rapid threat detection & emotion tagging.
    • Cerebellum: balance, timing, fine motor control.
    • Brainstem: breathing, heart rate, arousal.
    Quiz me on parts

    Memory types

    • Working: short-term “workspace”.
    • Episodic: events (time/place).
    • Semantic: facts/knowledge.
    • Procedural: skills & habits.
    Quiz me on memory

    Neurotransmitters

    • Dopamine: motivation & reward prediction.
    • Serotonin: mood, sleep, appetite.
    • Acetylcholine: attention, learning; neuromuscular junction.
    • GABA: main inhibitory transmitter.
    Quiz me on transmitters

    Sleep

    • NREM (deep) sleep: stabilizes & consolidates memories.
    • REM sleep: links ideas, processes emotion & creativity.
    • Circadian rhythm: ~24h body clock; light sets timing.
    • Habits: consistent schedule, limit caffeine, wind-down.

    Quick summaries

    Parts of the brain

    Frontal (plan/speak), Parietal (touch/space), Temporal (hearing/lang/memory), Occipital (vision), plus Hippocampus, Amygdala, Cerebellum, Brainstem.

    Practice quiz

    Memory types

    Working holds info briefly; Episodic = events; Semantic = facts; Procedural = skills. Sleep & spaced retrieval strengthen them.

    Practice quiz

    Neurotransmitters

    Dopamine (motivation/reward), Serotonin (mood/sleep), ACh (attention/learning), GABA (inhibition).

    Practice quiz

    Sleep

    Deep NREM stabilizes memories; REM links ideas & emotion. Keep a consistent schedule; light sets your 24-hour clock.

    Practice quiz

    Extra MCQ practice

    1. Which lobe is most involved in impulse control and planning? (Frontal)
    2. Which habit after studying most improves long-term recall? (Sleep + spaced retrieval)
    3. Anxiolytics that potentiate which transmitter increase inhibition? (GABA)
    4. Which memory stores the fact that Paris is the capital of France? (Semantic)
    5. Which sleep stage is most tied to creative recombination? (REM)
